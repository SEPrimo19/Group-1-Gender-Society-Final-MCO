Gender Roles in the
Philippine Context

Gender roles in the Philippines have evolved across different historical periods, shaped by culture, colonization, religion, and modern social changes.

Start the Journey

Historical Perspective

Pre-Colonial Period

Filipino societies were egalitarian.

  • Babaylan (often women or feminized men) held spiritual and political power.
  • Property and lineage could be matrilineal.
  • Women could own land, choose partners, and lead communities.

Spanish-Colonial Period

Introduction of strict gender binaries.

  • Emphasis on the Maria Clara ideal: modest, obedient, and domestic womanhood.
    • [cite_start]
  • Men were expected to be breadwinners, political leaders, and decision-makers. [cite: 414-415]

American Period

Western education opened opportunities for women.

  • Start of women's suffrage; [cite_start]Filipinas gained the right to vote in 1937. [cite: 419-421]
  • Emergence of women in professions (teachers, nurses, clerks).
